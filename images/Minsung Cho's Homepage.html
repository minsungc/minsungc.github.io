Hello!

I'm a first year Ph.D. student at the Programming Research Lab at Northeastern University, advised by Steven Holtzen.

I recently graduated from Carnegie Mellon University with a B.S. in mathematics and philosophy. I was advised by Jeremy Avigad.

My research is in programming languages and artificial intelligence. I use tools from automated reasoning to make probabilistic programming languages more robust, scalable, and efficient. I'm also interested in type systems and formalized mathematics, mostly via Lean.

In a past life I was a mathematician working in functional analysis. I have a publication from that epoch listed below.

I'm a first-generation Korean American. My Erdős number is 5. According to David Savitt's Springer GTM personality quiz, I am Mac Lane's Categories for the Working Mathematician.
